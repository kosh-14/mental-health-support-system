Frequently Asked Questions

1. How accurate is the information provided in this tool?

The information provided in this tool is gathered from reputable mental health organizations, medical institutions, and professional resources. However, it is intended for informational purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult with a qualified healthcare provider for personalized guidance.

2. Can I use this tool as a substitute for therapy?

No, this tool is not a substitute for professional therapy or medical treatment. It is designed to provide information, resources, and support, but cannot replace the personalized care and treatment that a qualified mental health professional can provide. If you're experiencing mental health challenges, we strongly encourage you to seek professional help.

3. How often is the information updated?

We strive to keep all information current and regularly review resources and helpline numbers. However, websites and contact information may change. We recommend verifying any external links or contact details before use. The general information about mental health conditions is based on established medical knowledge that evolves gradually.

4. Is my information kept private when using this tool?

This tool operates entirely in your browser and does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information. Your selections and navigation remain on your device. However, when you click on external website links, those sites will have their own privacy policies that you should review.

5. What should I do if I'm in crisis right now?

If you're in immediate crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself or others:

  • Call emergency services (911 in the US, 112 in many other countries)
  • Contact a crisis helpline (see the helplines page for numbers)
  • Go to the nearest emergency room
  • Contact a trusted friend, family member, or mental health professional

This tool is not designed for crisis intervention.

6. Can I suggest additional resources or report incorrect information?

While this is a static tool that doesn't have a backend for submissions, we recommend verifying information through multiple reputable sources. For the most accurate and up-to-date information, consult directly with mental health organizations, medical institutions, or healthcare providers in your area.

7. Why are there only six mental health conditions covered?

This tool focuses on six common mental health conditions to provide comprehensive information for each. There are many other mental health conditions that aren't covered here. If you're looking for information about a condition not included, we recommend consulting specialized mental health organizations or healthcare professionals who can provide appropriate resources and guidance.
